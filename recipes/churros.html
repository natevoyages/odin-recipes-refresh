Churros recipe

Description

Churros are a classic Spanish and Latin American fried pastry, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. They're typically coated in cinnamon sugar and often served with a rich chocolate dipping sauce. Perfect as a dessert or sweet snack.

Ingredients

For Churros

  • 1 cup (240ml) water
  • 2 ½ tablespoons (35g) sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) vegetable oil (or butter)
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For coating

  • ½ cup (100g) sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

(Optional) Chocolate dipping sauce:

  • ½ cup (120ml) heavy cream
  • 100g (about 3.5 oz) dark or semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)

Directions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine water, sugar, salt, and oil. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Once boiling, remove from heat and stir in flour all at once.
  3. Mix vigorously with a wooden spoon until a smooth dough forms and pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  4. Let the dough cool slightly, then transfer it to a piping bag fitted with a large star tip (for the classic churro ridges).
  5. Heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil in a deep pan to 350-375°F (175-190°C).
  6. Pipe 4-6 inch lengths of dough directly into the oil, cutting with scissors or a knife.
  7. Fry in batches until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  8. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  9. While warm, roll the churros in a mixture of cinnamon and sugar until evenly coated.
  10. (Optional) Heat cream in a small saucepan until just simmering.
  11. (Optional) Pour over chopped chocolate and let sit for 1 minute.
  12. (Optional) Stir until smooth and glossy. Sweeten if desired.
